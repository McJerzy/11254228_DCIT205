In the Information Technology age, a strong grasp of Computer Science is crucial for society to maximize the benefits of IT and drive economic progress. This field encompasses the study and development of applications, software systems, and ethical methodologies for reliable computer programming. Employers seek Computer Scientists proficient in programming, well-versed in current techniques, and capable of applying engineering principles to program development and maintenance. The department offers diverse courses, providing students with skills applicable across various economic sectors. Graduates are equipped for employment in the computing industry, contributing to project or research teams with specialized skills. The curriculum also serves as a foundation for further study or research by exposing students to established and emerging technologies and active research fields in Computer Science. Undergraduate degree programs include Bachelor of Science in Computer Science (Single-Major and Major-Minor options) and Bachelor of Science in Information Technology
